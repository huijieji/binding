RE THINKING AESTHETICS IN DESIGN

— Design by Huijie Ji

All three articles explore the deeper meaning and value in design and architecture, emphasizing that understanding history and context is essential for meaningful innovation. They argue for a critical, inclusive view of design, challenging traditional standards of beauty and simplicity.

01

KATE WAGNER

LOOKING AROUND:
ALL BUILDINGS ARE INTERESTING

This article by Kate Wagner advocates for a non-elitist view of architecture, suggesting that every building, regardless of perceived quality, holds value and interest. Wagner encourages readers to appreciate the diversity and stories within everyday architectural forms.

02

Kristen Coogan

Knowing Your Design History is Crucial to Aesthetic Innovation

Madeleine Morley stresses the importance of design history as a foundation for innovation, arguing that knowing past styles and movements empowers designers to create more informed, original work. The article highlights how history can inspire new design possibilities and prevent superficial trends.

03

Andrew Blauvelt

Towards a Complex Simplicity

Steven Heller discusses the balance between complexity and simplicity in design, explaining that achieving "simple" design often involves nuanced, thoughtful choices. He challenges the notion that simplicity is easy, suggesting instead that it requires a sophisticated understanding of design fundamentals.
